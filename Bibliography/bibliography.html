DeZure, Deborah, Matthew Kaplan, and Martha A. Deerman. "Research on student notetaking: Implications for faculty and graduate student instructors." CRLT Occasional Paper No 16 (2001). Many studies of notetaking find that review of notes (one's own, borrowed notes, or notes provided by the instructor) significantly improves recall of lecture material.

Summarization and Interaction Techniques for Enhancing the Learning Experience of How-to Videos (ToolScape)

  • Video summarization focusing on "how-to videos" in particular.
    Pilot test with different summary designs, users could not comprehend at all fast forward tutorial video at 64x.
  • Information intensive and structured.
  • Previous research almost unanimously supports that higher interactivity with instructional content aids learning [Ferguson and Hegarty 1995; Tversky et al. 2002]. Proactive and random access to video content based on queries or search targets. Tversky et al [2002] state that "stopping, starting and replaying an animation can allow reinspection", which in turn can mitigate the challenges in perception and comprehension and further facilitate learning.
  • [Palmiter and Elkerton 1991; Palmiter 1991] while users with animated demonstrations complete tasks faster with fewer errors, weaker performance in retention. [Harrison 1995] animated tutorials faster than textual tutorials, but no significant difference between still and animated graphics.
  • Tversky no decisive evidence in favor of animated instructions. [Hoffler and Leutner 2007] animated instructions show higher effect size.
  • Recent HCI negative position on video instructions, or advocate using them in context [Grabler 2009; Kelleher and Pausch 2005; Grossman and Fitzmaurice 2010; Pongnumkul 2011]
  • Recurring drawback of video instructions suggested by literature is pacing out of control
  • Mixed Format instructions: animations are good for physical procedures, while still images are good for conceptual processes [Clark and Mayer 2007].

Höffler, Tim N., and Detlev Leutner. "Instructional animation versus static pictures: A meta-analysis." Learning and instruction 17.6 (2007): 722-738.

  • A meta analysis of: when is animation more useful than stills? what are the moderator variables?
  • Video-based versus computer based. Level of realism. Representational and decorational animation. Annotation with text. Signaling cues such as highlight or arrows. Instructional domain. Type of knowledge: procedural, motor knowledge / declarative knowledge and problem-solving knowledge. Amount of learner time.
  • Representational animations are significantly superior than decorational. "An animation is liekly to be useful when the learning material entails motion, trajectory or change over time so that the animation helps to build a mental model of dynamics." (Betrancourt and Tversky, 2000)
  • Procedural knowledge >> Declarative >= Problem sovling
  • Video based animation ~= computer-based animation
  • Signaling cues, text: not conclusive. However, [Mayer and Gallini, 1990] state multimedia principle that static pictures + text are better than text alone.
