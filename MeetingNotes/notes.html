Properties we would like the Video Notes to have:

  • Self contained: First-time viewer should be able to understand content from the summary.
  • Hierarchical: User can choose level-of-detail
  • Structured: It would be nice if we could organize the concepts in the video into semantic units
  • Relations between concetps: Provde easy way to access relationship between contents, either within the video or between different videos
  • If we can identify important concepts or semantic units in the video, we may be able to provide tools to create a quiz, or appropriate search queries.
  • Easy to edit, or add on top on whatever augmentation we provide.

Observations about video lectures and process of making Video Notes

  • Drawings overlayed on top of each other. Emphasizing or tagging part of images. Completely static summary is limited in conveying this kind of sequence of events.
  • Including entire transcript is way too crowded.
  • Transcript while drawing tends to be repetitive (at least in Khan Academy), describing the drawing itself.
  • Some mistakes are erased quickly and do not appear on the final board
  • Some mistakes are noticed only after recording and is left on the final board. Khan has a popup at the right corner of the video correcting these mistakes.
  • Matching corresponding visual parts and transcripts is an imporant part of creating the notes.
  • Operations inside video lectures:
    1. Pointing
    2. drawing or writing
    3. highlighting (MIT201XXSP13-J010300_100, 2:16) or underlining
    4. Rescaling and re-positioning objects
    5. Copy and pasting
    6. Scrolling between screens
    7. New page (either by erasing, or by turning to a new page)
    8. pointing to already drawn object
    9. Striking parts of board
    10. Explanation
    11. Insert iamge
    12. Drawing over a previous image
    13. Explaining side concepts
  • Illustrations can have progression
  • Illustrations have correspondence

    • Observation about illustrations

    • Making correspondence clear with matching colors, explicit connection with dotted lines, labeling
      e.g.
    • When an object is duplicated, labeling helps to identfiy which objects are same
    • Make the duplicate object as close as possible with the original object, in terms of size, color, shape.
